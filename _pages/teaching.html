Teaching

I have taught and supported undergraduate and postgraduate modules in computer vision, image processing, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.

Current and Recent Teaching

  • INT302 Image Processing
    This module introduces fundamental theories and practical techniques in image processing, including image enhancement, filtering, edge detection, segmentation, feature extraction, and image analysis.

  • INT305 Machine Learning
    This module covers core concepts and methods in machine learning, including supervised learning, unsupervised learning, model evaluation, neural networks, and practical machine learning applications.

Student Supervision

I welcome motivated undergraduate and postgraduate students who are interested in computer vision, weakly supervised learning, visual foundation models, industrial anomaly detection, point cloud understanding, and multimodal perception.

Supervision Topics

  • Weakly supervised semantic segmentation
  • Visual foundation models for dense prediction
  • Open-vocabulary and open-world visual perception
  • Few-shot and training-free industrial anomaly detection
  • 3D point cloud understanding
  • Multimodal visual perception
