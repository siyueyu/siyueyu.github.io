My research focuses on computer vision and artificial intelligence, with an emphasis on low-annotation-cost visual learning, foundation-model-driven dense prediction, and efficient visual understanding in open and complex scenarios.

## Research Interests

### Weakly Supervised Visual Learning

I study weakly supervised learning methods for semantic segmentation and dense prediction. The goal is to reduce the dependence on pixel-level annotations while maintaining strong segmentation performance.

### Visual Foundation Models

I am interested in adapting and analyzing visual foundation models, such as CLIP and DINO, for dense visual understanding tasks, including weakly supervised semantic segmentation, open-vocabulary segmentation, and multimodal perception.

### Industrial Anomaly Detection

I work on few-shot and training-free anomaly detection methods for industrial inspection, aiming to improve robustness and efficiency under limited defect samples.

### 3D Scene Understanding

I am also interested in point cloud understanding and multimodal scene perception, particularly for safety-critical scenarios such as autonomous driving and railway environments.

## Representative Projects

- Foundation-model-driven weakly supervised semantic segmentation
- Open-world and open-vocabulary visual perception
- Few-shot industrial anomaly detection
- Multimodal perception for railway safety
